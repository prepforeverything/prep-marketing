# Copy Formulas

## Quick-Reference Selection Table

| Situation | Best Formula | Funnel Stage |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Cold audience, awareness content | AIDA | TOFU |
| Known pain point, solution sell | PAS | MOFU |
| Transformation-focused offer | BAB | MOFU/BOFU |
| Feature-heavy product | FAB | MOFU |
| Persuasion-heavy sales page | 4Ps | BOFU |
| Urgency-driven offer or email | 4Us | TOFU/BOFU |
| Educational content with offer | QUEST | TOFU/MOFU |
| Story-driven testimonial or case study | Star-Story-Solution | MOFU/BOFU |

---

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Structure**
1. Attention — grab with a bold headline, stat, or question
2. Interest — relevant detail that keeps them reading
3. Desire — paint the outcome; connect to their goal
4. Action — single clear CTA

**Funnel stage:** TOFU (primary), MOFU

**Example:** "Still paying for tools you don't use? [Attention] Most SaaS stacks have 40% redundancy. [Interest] Teams that audit quarterly save an average of $18k/year. [Desire] Run your free audit in 10 minutes. [Action]"

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

**Structure**
1. Problem — name the pain clearly
2. Agitate — deepen the sting; show consequences of inaction
3. Solution — position the offer as the way out

**Funnel stage:** MOFU (primary), TOFU

**Example:** "Your onboarding emails are getting ignored. [Problem] Every day new users churn before they see value — and you can't get that revenue back. [Agitate] Our onboarding sequence builder turns activation into a system. [Solution]"

---

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Structure**
1. Before — describe current frustrating state
2. After — describe the transformed state
3. Bridge — show how the product closes that gap

**Funnel stage:** MOFU/BOFU

**Example:** "Before: 14 spreadsheets and a prayer. After: one dashboard, live data, zero Monday morning panic. Bridge: Migrate your reporting stack in one afternoon."

---

## FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits

**Structure**
1. Feature — what it does (the thing)
2. Advantage — why that's better than the alternative
3. Benefit — what the user gains (the outcome)

**Funnel stage:** MOFU

**Example:** "256-bit encryption [Feature] — stronger than the standard banks use [Advantage] — so your client data is protected even if you lose a device [Benefit]."

---

## 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

**Structure**
1. Promise — one bold benefit statement up front
2. Picture — vivid scenario of life with the benefit
3. Proof — evidence (stat, testimonial, case study)
4. Push — urgent CTA

**Funnel stage:** BOFU

**Example:** "Cut reporting time in half. [Promise] Imagine Fridays freed from spreadsheet cleanup. [Picture] Acme Corp reclaimed 6 hours per analyst per week in 30 days. [Proof] Start your trial before Friday. [Push]"

---

## 4Us — Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-Specific

**Structure** (applied to headline or subject line)
1. Useful — delivers clear value
2. Urgent — time or scarcity pressure
3. Unique — differentiates from expected claims
4. Ultra-Specific — concrete numbers or details

**Funnel stage:** TOFU (headlines/subject lines), BOFU (CTAs)

**Example headline:** "The 3-step audit that recovered $22k in 48 hours — only 12 spots left this month."

---

## QUEST — Qualify, Understand, Educate, Stimulate, Transition

**Structure**
1. Qualify — filter for the right reader immediately
2. Understand — show you get their world
3. Educate — give them something genuinely useful
4. Stimulate — introduce the possibility of a better outcome
5. Transition — move them toward the offer naturally

**Funnel stage:** TOFU/MOFU (long-form content, email sequences)

**Example:** "If you manage a remote team of 10+... [Qualify] you already know the coordination tax is real. [Understand] Here's the async meeting structure 200 remote-first teams use. [Educate] What if you could run your whole sprint review in one Loom? [Stimulate] Our async playbook shows you exactly how. [Transition]"

---

## Star-Story-Solution

**Structure**
1. Star — introduce a protagonist (customer, brand, persona)
2. Story — tell their struggle and journey
3. Solution — reveal the turning point and outcome

**Funnel stage:** MOFU/BOFU (case studies, testimonials, video scripts)

**Example:** "Meet Priya, head of ops at a 50-person agency. [Star] Last April she was manually reconciling 300 invoices a week — until one Friday the whole system broke. [Story] She implemented AutoPay in a weekend. Three months later, reconciliation is automated and she runs her team 10 hours leaner per week. [Solution]"

---

## Formula Decision Notes

- Stack formulas when appropriate: use AIDA for page structure, PAS for a body section, 4Us for the headline.
- QUEST is best for newsletter content or cold email sequences where trust must be built before the ask.
- BAB and Star-Story-Solution both work as video scripts — BAB is faster; SSS lands harder.
- FAB is frequently skipped; use it to prevent writing features-only bullet lists.
