# Exam & Admission Calendar — cross-market demand seasonality

> **Why this file exists.** Test-prep demand is the #1 thing a marketing calendar must respect, yet
> it is easy to get backwards. This file holds the **cross-market** framing; the **per-market intent
> windows** (the dated specifics) live in each `markets/<m>.md` under "Exam cycle & intent windows".
>
> **Governance:** the *patterns* below are stable public knowledge and safe to plan against. **Exact
> dates shift every cycle — they are NOT verified facts.** Before any dated exam/admission claim
> reaches customer copy, confirm it via `/mkt-research` (which writes a PROPOSAL with a source · as-of)
> and, if it is an outcome/eligibility claim, register it as an approved `claim_id`. Never publish a
> specific date or deadline from this file as fact.

## The core principle (evergreen — this is the strategy)

IELTS itself is offered **year-round** (multiple sessions per month in major cities via IDP / British
Council; Academic and General; computer-delivered results in days, paper in ~2 weeks — *verify current
turnaround per cycle*). So demand is **not** driven by the test date — the test is almost always
available. Demand is driven by the **deadline the learner needs the score for**:

- **University admission cycles** (the biggest driver — see each market below).
- **Scholarship / study-abroad application deadlines** (most fall intakes apply the *prior* autumn–winter).
- **Visa / enrolment timelines** for departing students.
- **The school calendar** — learners have time to study in **summer** and just **after major holidays**.

**Planning rule:** work **backwards** from the learner's deadline by the **course length**. A 12-week
course aimed at an August admission deadline must acquire its leads by **May**. Run acquisition to
*lead* the deadline by (course length + decision time), not on the deadline itself — by then it is too
late to enrol and the intent has converted elsewhere.

## How to use this in planning

- Every editorial calendar and campaign plan must state the **exam-intent window** each major push
  targets (the `marketing-content-strategy` skill requires a season/exam-window column).
- A piece that targets "admission urgency" only works **inside** the lead-the-deadline window — outside
  it, lead with the evergreen goal-band motivation instead (see [`brand-voice.md`](brand-voice.md):
  Students "lead with the goal band and the deadline").
- Locale ≠ market: do not copy VN's calendar onto TH/TW/ID/HK — each admission system differs. Verify
  per market via `/mkt-research`.
