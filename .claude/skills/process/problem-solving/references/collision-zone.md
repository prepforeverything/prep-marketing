# Collision-Zone Thinking

Deliberate metaphor-mixing from unrelated domains to generate breakthrough ideas.

## Process

1. **Pick an unrelated domain** — electrical circuits, postal mail, water flow, urban planning, biology, music
2. **Force the metaphor** — "What if we treated [our problem] like [domain concept]?"
3. **Explore emergent properties** — What does the metaphor suggest that we haven't considered?
4. **Test boundaries** — Where does the metaphor break down? The breakdown often reveals the real constraint.
5. **Extract insight** — What concrete design decision does this suggest?

## Reference Collisions

- Electrical circuits → circuit breakers (failure isolation)
- Postal mail → message queues (async delivery, guaranteed delivery)
- Water flow → streaming (backpressure, overflow handling)
- Urban planning → microservices (zoning, infrastructure, traffic routing)
- Biology → self-healing systems (immune response, redundancy)

## When to Use

- Conventional approaches have been exhausted
- The problem space feels too familiar (you're stuck in local optima)
- You need a fundamentally different perspective
