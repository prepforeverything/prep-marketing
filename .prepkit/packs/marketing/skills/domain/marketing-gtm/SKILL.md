# Marketing GTM

Plans launches and go-to-market. Use when planning a product launch or go-to-market — launch
sequencing, competitive framing, and sales enablement.

## When To Use

- Planning a course/product/feature launch
- Framing differentiation against named competitors
- Building sales-enablement / consultant assets for a launch
- Shaping the message for a pricing or packaging change

## Rules

1. A launch is a sequence, not an event: pre-launch → launch → post-launch, each with owners,
   assets, and a checkpoint.
2. Lead with one core message; everything else supports it. Decide the single success metric before
   planning tactics.
3. Competitive claims are claims — every comparison is sourced and dated in
   `context/competitors.md`; default to honest, specific differentiation over superlatives.
4. Never invent a price; pricing messaging maps to approved claims (CLM-003..005). Sell the tier
   story and value, not just the number.
5. Equip the front line: a launch without FAQ, objection handling, and a consultant script leaks
   demand at the point of sale.
6. Separate leading indicators (signups, consults booked) from lagging ones (enrolments, revenue)
   when setting targets.

## Output Format

A launch narrative + the core message; a phased plan (pre/launch/post → owner → asset →
checkpoint); a competitive differentiation table; a sales-enablement pack; success metrics.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating launch as a one-day announcement with no pre- or post- phase.
- Differentiating with superlatives ("the best") instead of sourced specifics.
- Shipping a launch with no sales-enablement assets for consultants.
- Quoting competitor prices or features without a dated source.
- Setting a single revenue target with no leading indicator to steer by.

## Gotchas

- Most launches fail at the front line, not the campaign — the consultant script and objection handling matter more than the hero banner.
- Competitor facts go stale fast; an undated comparison is a liability, not an asset.
- A second "re-launch" wave to non-openers often outperforms the original send — plan the post-launch phase up front.
- Pricing perception is relative; anchor the recommended tier against the others rather than presenting prices in isolation.
- Demand with no capture path (consult booking, Zalo, form) is wasted spend — wire the conversion path before you drive traffic.
