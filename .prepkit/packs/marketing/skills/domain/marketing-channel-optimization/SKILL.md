# Marketing Channel Optimization

## When To Use

- Selecting distribution channels for a campaign or product launch
- Allocating budget across paid and organic channels
- Planning cross-channel coordination and content adaptation
- Evaluating whether a channel is worth continued investment

## Rules

1. Check `marketing-product-context` output before gathering context that may already exist.
2. Select channels from audience data — where the audience actually is — not from habit or personal preference.
3. Validate organic performance before paid amplification — paid scales what already works; it does not replace organic signal.
4. Classify every channel by type before investing: Owned (email, blog, community), Rented (social, app stores, YouTube), or Borrowed (guest content, influencer, speaking). Each type has distinct risk and leverage properties.
5. Funnel rented and borrowed attention toward owned channels — email capture, community join, subscribe. Discovery on rented channels is the starting point, not the destination.
6. Define a success metric per channel before investing; treat any channel without a defined success metric as a time-limited experiment.
7. Adapt content format and tone to each platform — do not repurpose the same asset unchanged across all channels.
8. Design for mobile-dominant distribution by default — approximately 70% of discovery happens on mobile.

## Platform-Specific Norms

- LinkedIn: rewards professional insight and longer-form posts
- Instagram: rewards visual polish and high production quality
- TikTok: demands a hook in the first 3 seconds; format-first content wins

## Anti-patterns

- Repurposing the same asset unchanged across all platforms
- Running paid campaigns before any organic content has been validated
- Treating channels as silos with no cross-channel funnel strategy
- Designing campaigns desktop-first and retrofitting mobile
- Adding channels because they are trending rather than because the audience is there

## Gotchas

- Do not activate this skill for campaign brief or copy decisions — use it when channel selection, budget split, or distribution strategy is the explicit question.
- Rented channels (social media) are algorithmically controlled — reach is not owned and can disappear overnight; never build a marketing strategy that depends entirely on rented reach.
- Paid amplification of unvalidated content burns budget and reveals nothing useful; validate organic signal first.
- Platform-specific adaptation is not cosmetic — the same message framed identically on LinkedIn and TikTok will fail on at least one of them.
- Attribution between channels is imperfect; over-crediting last-touch and under-crediting upper-funnel channels is the default outcome without intentional UTM discipline.
