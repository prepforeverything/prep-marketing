# Company

PrepEdu (brand: **Prep**) is an AI-powered EdTech company focused on **test preparation** —
IELTS, TOEIC, HSK, PTE, TOEFL, and VSTEP. We help learners reach their target scores with
AI-driven scoring and adaptive learning.

- **Scale:** 500K+ users.
- **Revenue:** $9.3M (2025), targeting $15–18M (2026), CAGR ~44%.

> Internal strategy reference. Safe for the team to read. External-facing numbers (user
> counts, revenue) must be approved via `claims.json` before they appear in marketing.

## Markets

| Code | Market | Key trait |
|------|--------|-----------|
| VN | Vietnam | Largest base; price-sensitive; mobile-first |
| TH | Thailand | Growing; significant content QC gap; **PDPA** applies |
| ID | Indonesia | High volume; connectivity varies |
| TW | Taiwan | Mature; high digital literacy |
| KR | Korea | Planned; premium and competitive (Hackers, YBM) |
| HK | Hong Kong | Bilingual; exam-driven |

**Cross-market rule:** any decision affecting customer experience must be sanity-checked
across markets. What works in VN may fail in TH/ID due to connectivity, price sensitivity,
language, or regulation. VN ≠ TH ≠ ID — always state which market a campaign is for.

## Squads & North Stars

| Squad | Owns | North Star |
|-------|------|------------|
| Learning | Core test-taking + AI scoring | **WALI** (Weekly Active Learners Intensive: 3+ sessions/week) |
| Growth | Acquisition, activation, SEO, landing pages, Dictionary | WNAU (Weekly New Active Users reaching first value) |
| B2B | Enterprise clients, teacher dashboards, licenses | CHS (Client Health Score 0–100) |
| Platform | Auth, payment, infra, shared services | WAU + system reliability |
| Learning Utilities | Dictionary, vocab apps, innovation lab | WAU (PLG vehicle) |
| AI (HN/DN) | Scoring engines, adaptive learning | Scoring reliability + latency SLOs |

> **WALI is the primary company North Star** — depth of engagement, not mere presence.
> Marketing campaigns should connect to a North Star; acquisition work usually serves WNAU.

## Strategic Constraints (keep these in mind for every campaign)

1. **IELTS dependency > 40% of revenue** → the strategy is to diversify *below* 40%. Flag any
   campaign that inadvertently deepens IELTS concentration instead of broadening the mix.
2. **PLG transformation** — moving from sales-led (CAC ~$250) to product-led
   (target CAC < $65, free-to-paid 12–15%, activation 50–60%). Favour low-CAC, self-serve
   acquisition motions.
3. **Mobile-first** — the majority of access is on mobile. Design copy and assets for small
   screens first.
4. **Multi-market localization** — 6+ markets; localization cost and quality control are a
   real constraint, not an afterthought.

> The biggest current growth bottleneck is **activation, not retention** (retention is
> already strong). Campaigns should move users to their first value moment, not just to a
> signup.
