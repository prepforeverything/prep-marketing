# Brand Voice Framework

## Voice Attributes

Define 3–5 adjectives that capture the brand's personality. Each attribute should be distinct and testable — you should be able to point to copy and say "this is or isn't [attribute]."

| Attribute | What it means in practice | What it does NOT mean |
|-----------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| [e.g., Bold] | Direct claims, no hedging, confident assertions | Aggressive, dismissive, arrogant |
| [e.g., Precise] | Specific numbers, named features, concrete outcomes | Technical jargon, pedantic detail |
| [e.g., Warm] | Addresses reader as a person, acknowledges frustration | Sycophantic, overly casual |

---

## Tone Spectrum Per Context

Tone shifts by context; voice (personality) stays constant.

| Context | Formal–Casual | Serious–Playful | Notes |
|---------|--------------|----------------|-------|
| Support / error messages | Formal | Serious | Clarity over personality; never flippant about user pain |
| Product UI (labels, CTAs) | Casual | Neutral | Short, action-oriented, no decoration |
| Marketing / landing pages | Neutral | Slight playfulness | Lead with outcome, let voice show in word choice |
| Email campaigns | Casual | Neutral–playful | Conversational opener, structured body |
| Social media | Casual | Playful | Voice most visible; still substantive |

## Vocabulary Do / Don't

| Instead of | Use | Why |
|------------|-----|-----|
| Utilize | Use | Simpler without losing precision |
| Leverage | Use / apply / tap | "Leverage" is overused and often vague |
| Seamlessly | [specific benefit] | Claims without evidence; replace with outcome |
| Cutting-edge / next-gen | [specific capability] | Undefined; prove it, don't label it |
| Solutions | [product name] / [what it does] | Generic; buyers tune it out |
| Synergy | [describe the actual combination] | Jargon; readers stop trusting copy |
| [Add brand-specific terms] | | |

## Sentence Style Guidelines

- **Length**: Default to short (10–18 words). Break long thoughts into two sentences.
- **Structure**: Lead with the benefit or action, then add context. Avoid throat-clearing openers ("In today's world…").
- **Active over passive**: "We ship updates daily" not "Updates are shipped daily."
- **Second person**: Address the reader as "you." Avoid "users" or "customers" in copy directed at them.
- **Punctuation**: Prefer em dashes over parentheses for asides. Avoid exclamation marks except in genuine moments of celebration.
- **Numbers**: Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above. Numerals are stronger in headlines.

## Examples Per Channel

Same message — "Our product automatically categorizes your support tickets" — adapted by channel:

| Channel | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Email subject | Your tickets, sorted automatically — no setup needed |
| Landing page headline | Stop sorting tickets manually. [Product] does it for you. |
| Product UI tooltip | Tickets are auto-categorized. Edit any label in one click. |
| Social (X/LinkedIn) | Spent 2 hours sorting tickets last week? We fixed that. |
| Error message | Couldn't categorize this ticket automatically. Review and assign it manually. |

## Voice Audit Checklist

Run this against any draft before publishing:

- [ ] Would we say this out loud in a conversation with a customer?
- [ ] Does this sound like us, or like a generic SaaS brand?
- [ ] Have we replaced all "solutions," "leverage," and "seamlessly" occurrences?
- [ ] Is every claim specific — a number, a named feature, or a concrete outcome?
- [ ] Is the sentence structure active rather than passive?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the channel this copy lives in?
- [ ] Does the opening sentence lead with the reader's benefit, not our feature?
- [ ] Are all brand-restricted words (see vocabulary table) absent?
- [ ] Is the reading level appropriate for the audience (aim for grade 8–10 for broad audiences)?
- [ ] If stripped of logo and brand name, would this copy still feel distinctly like us?
