# Email Copy

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Subject line impact on opens | 47% of recipients decide based on subject line alone | Source: OptinMonster / HubSpot |
| Personalization lift | 26% higher open rates with personalized subject lines | Source: Campaign Monitor |
| Mobile email opens | 46–60% of all email opens | Always test mobile rendering |
| CTA click rate | 2–5% avg; above 10% is strong | Depends heavily on list quality |
| Plain text vs HTML | Plain text often outperforms in cold outreach | HTML performs better for nurture |
| Optimal email send time | Tuesday–Thursday, 10am or 2pm recipient timezone | Test for your specific list |

## Subject Line Formulas

### Curiosity Gap

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| The [adjective] truth about [topic] | The surprising truth about open rates |
| What most [audience] get wrong about [topic] | What most founders get wrong about pricing |
| I didn't expect this to work — but it did | I didn't expect this to work — but it did |
| [Outcome] — here's the part nobody talks about | More leads — here's the part nobody talks about |

### Benefit-Driven

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| How to [outcome] in [timeframe] | How to double replies in 7 days |
| [N] ways to [benefit] this [timeframe] | 5 ways to reduce churn this quarter |
| [Outcome] without [pain] | Better copy without starting from scratch |

### Question

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Are you making this [topic] mistake? | Are you making this segmentation mistake? |
| Is your [thing] really [adjective]? | Is your onboarding really working? |
| What would [outcome] mean for you? | What would 30% more opens mean for you? |

### Urgency

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| [Offer] ends [day/date] | Free audit offer ends Friday |
| Last chance: [benefit] | Last chance: lock in your rate |
| [N] spots left for [thing] | 4 spots left for the October cohort |

### Personal

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Quick question, [first name] | Quick question, Sarah |
| I noticed [their thing] | I noticed your pricing page changed |
| [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out | Alex Chen suggested I reach out |

### Number-Based

| Template | Example |
|----------|---------|
| [N] [things] for [audience] | 3 subject line fixes for B2B marketers |
| The [N]-minute fix for [problem] | The 10-minute fix for low open rates |

## Preview Text Strategy

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Never duplicate the subject line | Preview text is a second hook, not a repeat |
| Target length | 40–90 characters; 85–100 with preheader |
| Extend the curiosity | If subject line asks a question, preview starts the answer |
| Add specificity | If subject line is broad, preview adds a number or name |
| CTA in preview | Works for promotional emails with a clear offer |
| Emoji placement | One emoji at start of preview to stand out in inbox (optional) |

## Email Body Structures

### Short Format (50–100 words)

Best for: cold outreach, re-engagement, transactional

```
Hook line (1 sentence — names the relevant pain or situation)
Bridge (1–2 sentences — shows you understand their context)
Value or ask (1–2 sentences — what you're offering or requesting)
CTA (1 line — one action)
```

### Standard Format (100–200 words)

Best for: nurture, product updates, announcements

```
Hook (subject line callback or new angle — 1 sentence)
Problem / context (2–3 sentences)
Solution or value (2–3 sentences)
Proof or evidence (1 sentence with stat, quote, or example)
CTA (1–2 lines)
[Optional P.S.]
```

### Story Format (300–800 words)

Best for: newsletter, case study delivery, deep nurture

```
Opening story hook (1–3 sentences setting a scene)
Conflict / tension (2–3 sentences)
Insight or turn (the lesson or shift)
Value delivery (the main content — list, framework, or narrative)
Connection to offer (1 paragraph bridging to product)
CTA (explicit, low friction)
P.S. (often the second most-read element — use it)
```

## Email CTA Variations

| Context | CTA Approach |
|---------|-------------|
| Cold outreach | One low-friction ask: "Worth a 15-minute chat?" |
| Lead nurture | Resource-linked: "Read the full case study" |
| Product email | Trial or demo: "Start your free trial" |
| Promotional | Benefit + urgency: "Claim your 20% discount" |
| Newsletter | Soft: "Reply and let me know what you think" |
| Re-engagement | Yes/No: "Still interested? Click here. Want to unsubscribe? Click here." |
| Sequence final email | Direct: "Book a call or this will be my last email" |

## P.S. Strategies

The P.S. is often the second or third most-read element after the subject line and first line.

| P.S. Type | When to Use | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Reinforce the CTA | Always in promotional emails | P.S. The offer expires at midnight Friday. |
| Add a bonus | Increase perceived value | P.S. Reply to this email to get the bonus template. |
| Handle an objection | When hesitation is likely | P.S. No setup fees, no contracts — cancel anytime. |
| Add social proof | BOFU sequences | P.S. Over 2,400 teams chose Acme last quarter. |
| Personal note | Cold outreach | P.S. I genuinely found your talk on [topic] useful — that's why I reached out. |

## Email Sequence Types

### Onboarding Sequence

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 — Welcome | Immediately | Confirm value, deliver first win |
| 2 — Quick win | Day 1–2 | One action that creates immediate value |
| 3 — Feature spotlight | Day 3–4 | Introduce key feature tied to primary use case |
| 4 — Social proof | Day 5–7 | Case study or testimonial |
| 5 — Check-in / help | Day 8–10 | Surface support, reduce churn |
| 6 — Upgrade nudge | Day 14+ | Introduce paid tier or upsell |

### Nurture Sequence

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 — Value piece | Week 1 | Deliver promised lead magnet or insight |
| 2 — Education | Week 2 | Teach something useful; reinforce authority |
| 3 — Case study | Week 3 | Build desire via proof |
| 4 — Soft offer | Week 4 | Introduce offer with low-friction CTA |
| 5 — Objection handling | Week 5 | Address the most common hesitation |
| 6 — Direct offer | Week 6 | Clear ask with urgency or bonus |

### Re-engagement Sequence

| Email | Goal |
|-------|------|
| 1 — "We miss you" | Acknowledge inactivity; offer value |
| 2 — Best-of content | Surface top-performing resource |
| 3 — Yes or no | Binary choice to stay on list or unsubscribe |

### Cold Outreach Sequence

| Email | Goal |
|-------|------|
| 1 — Opening | Personal hook, relevant insight, one ask |
| 2 — Follow-up (Day 3) | Add value (resource, stat, perspective) |
| 3 — Different angle (Day 7) | New reason or angle, shorter than first |
| 4 — Break-up (Day 14) | "Last email" with permission to close loop |
